Young (Dung) Truong

Swartz Center for Computational Neuroscience · Institute for Neural Computation · UCSD

I am a researcher at the Swartz Center for Computational Neuroscience (SCCN) at the University of California, San Diego. My research focuses on applying deep learning and machine learning methods to electroencephalography (EEG) data, with the goal of developing scalable, generalizable foundation models for neural signal processing.

I co-organized the NeurIPS 2025 EEG Foundation Challenge, a large-scale competition on cross-task and cross-subject EEG decoding accepted to the NeurIPS 2025 Competition Track. Beyond research, I build and maintain open-source neuroscience infrastructure — from web platforms (NEMAR, EEGDash) to data processing toolkits (EEGLAB, Braindecode) — that serve thousands of researchers worldwide.

Research Interests

My work spans several areas including deep learning for EEG, self-supervised representation learning, neuroscience research infrastructure, and open-source scientific computing. I am particularly interested in developing EEG foundation models that generalize across tasks, subjects, and datasets, as well as building the open-access platforms and data pipelines that make large-scale neuroscience research accessible and reproducible.
